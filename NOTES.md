## Investment Observation — April 20, 2026
Wine as an investment shows strong CAGR (10.2%) but suffers from high illiquidity premium. Storage and insurance costs compress net returns.

## Authentication Premium
PSA 10 graded cards command 3-10x premium over raw cards. Grading costs ($20-$150/card) must be factored into ROI calculations. Only cards with estimated PSA 10 value >$200 are economically viable to grade.

## Pokémon Card Deep Dive — April 24, 2026

Analyzed PSA population report data for Base Set Charizard (PSA 10). Population has grown from 121 in 2020 to 3,847 in 2025, a 31x increase. Despite population growth, PSA 10 prices remain elevated at ~$350K, suggesting demand is outpacing supply even at higher grades.

## Market Correlation — April 28, 2026
Testing correlation between collectibles returns and S&P 500. High-end art and watches show low correlation (r=0.18), making them genuine diversifiers. Sports cards show moderate correlation (r=0.42) — more tied to consumer sentiment.

## Risk Framework Refinement — April 30, 2026
Adding 'cultural permanence score' to the collectibles framework. Factors: IP longevity, nostalgia demographic size, scarcity mechanism, and institutional recognition. Cards/items scoring high on cultural permanence show lower volatility and stronger floor prices.

## Liquidity Premium Analysis — May 01, 2026
Modeling the liquidity discount for illiquid collectibles. Wine and art require 6-18 months to liquidate at fair value — applying a 15-20% liquidity discount to quoted returns. Sports cards and Pokémon are more liquid (eBay/TCGPlayer markets) — 7-14 day liquidation at 90-95% of fair value. Liquidity-adjusted returns change the ranking significantly.

## May 2026 Update

Investigated sports card market cycles. 1990s overproduction era (Donruss, Score, Topps) remains largely worthless. Pre-1980 vintage cards (PSA 8+) show consistent 8–12% CAGR with low volatility.
